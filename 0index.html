About Me

I’m a first‑year student majoring in Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering at the University of New Haven. I’m motivated by the potential of molecular‑level engineering to improve health outcomes, and I enjoy taking early ideas and turning them into practical, testable plans with a team.

I chose this field because I want to help design medicines that are more effective, have fewer side effects, and are more affordable. I’m particularly interested in genetic engineering—how engineered molecules can enhance drug performance, safety, and accessibility. Alongside foundational chemistry and engineering coursework, I’m taking AIML 2450 to learn how AI can support research and technical documentation.

My current experiences include an introductory lab where I synthesized soap and documented reaction steps and safety practices, plus a sustainability proposal exercise that strengthened my teamwork and communication. Peers often describe me as curious, creative, and collaborative—a teammate who asks good questions, learns quickly, and communicates clearly.
